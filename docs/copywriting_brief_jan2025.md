# Copywriting Brief: January 2025 Sequences

**Date:** December 29, 2024
**Objective:** Write 3 new email sequences (A/B/C tier) for 5k new HVAC leads
**Constraint:** Must follow winning formula from original campaigns (50% opens, 103 clicks)

---

## What Worked (KEEP)

### The Winning Opening Hook
```
"How responsive is your CPA during busy season?"
```
This question opened EVERY tier's first email and drove 50%+ open rates. It's direct, pain-focused, and universally relatable.

### The Pain Pattern (Email 2 - highest click rate at 12%)
```
→ CPA goes MIA during busy season
→ Books are 3 months behind
→ April brings surprise tax bills
```
This pattern appears in bullets/arrows in follow-ups. It validates their frustration.

### Personalization That Worked
- **A-tier:** `"You're running {{service_software}}"` + `"{{review_count}} reviews doesn't happen by accident"`
- **B-tier:** `"Saw {{company}} on Google"` + `"{{review_count}} reviews"`
- **C-tier:** Minimal personalization, pure pain focus

### Tone & Structure
- Owner-to-owner conversational tone
- Short emails (80-120 words)
- No accounting jargon
- Trades language ("jobs", "busy season", "calls")
- Clear Calendly CTA in every email
- Subject lines: Simple, direct, often use {{first_name}} or {{company}}

### Subject Lines That Worked
- `{{first_name}} - tax season question`
- `Saw {{company}} on Google`
- `Question about your CPA`
- `Re: Quick question about {{company}}'s accounting` (follow-up)
- `Your tech stack vs. your CPA`
- `Last note about {{company}}`

---

## What Failed (AVOID)

### The "Scorecard" Pitch (A/B Tests - 0 Clicks)
Four variants tested in December all flopped:
- M&A Variant: 33.6% opens, **0 clicks**
- 35/100 Variant: 34.4% opens, **0 clicks**
- Social Proof: 39.2% opens, **0 clicks**
- Direct Variant: 24.4% opens, **0 clicks**

**Why it failed:**
- Too consultative/analytical
- Lost the direct pain-point urgency
- Sounded like a sales pitch, not peer advice

### Things to Avoid
- Complex value propositions
- "Scorecard" or audit language
- Generic business advice
- Long emails (150+ words)
- Multiple CTAs per email
- "Sound familiar?" closing (became stale)
- "Worth a conversation?" (weak CTA)
